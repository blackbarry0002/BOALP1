Your Privacy Rights

  • Right to Access: You can request and access all personal information Bank of America holds about you
  • Right to Correct: You can request corrections to inaccurate personal information in your account
  • Right to Delete: You can request deletion of personal information (subject to legal and regulatory requirements)
  • Right to Opt-Out: You can opt out of marketing communications and data sharing with third parties
  • Right to Data Portability: You can request your data in a commonly used electronic format
  • Right to Know: You have the right to know what information is collected and how it's used
  • Right to Non-Discrimination: Bank of America will not discriminate against you for exercising your privacy rights

Information Sharing Practices

What Information We Share:

Bank of America may share your personal and financial information with:

  • Bank of America affiliates and subsidiaries
  • Service providers who assist us (with strict confidentiality agreements)
  • Credit bureaus and other financial institutions (as required)
  • Law enforcement agencies (when legally required)
  • Other parties (only with your explicit consent)
What Information We Protect:

We do not sell your personal information to third parties. Bank of America protects the privacy of:

  • Social Security Number
  • Account numbers and transaction history
  • Financial profile and net worth information
  • Creditworthiness and credit score
  • Medical and insurance information

California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA)

California Residents - Your Rights

If you are a California resident, you have specific rights under the California Consumer Privacy Act:

  • Right to know what personal information is collected
  • Right to delete personal information (with exceptions)
  • Right to opt-out of "sales" of personal information
  • Right to correct inaccurate information
  • Right to data portability

Online Tracking

Cookies and Tracking Technologies: Bank of America uses cookies and similar technologies to enhance your experience, analyze site usage, and personalize content. You can control cookie settings through your browser preferences.
Do Not Track (DNT): Some browsers include a DNT feature. Bank of America acknowledges DNT signals, but your web experience may be limited if you enable DNT.
